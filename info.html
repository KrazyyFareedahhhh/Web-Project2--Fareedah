What Are Treaties?

A treaty is a special agreement between groups or countries. It’s like a promise to share, protect, or work together. In Canada, we have 5 different treaties. Today, let’s learn about Treaty 3!

Learn About Treaty 3

In 1873, Treaty 3 was made between the Anishinaabe people and the Canadian government. The Anishinaabe lived on the land for thousands of years, caring for nature and each other.

The government wanted to build on this land, so they asked for permission from the Anishinaabe. The Anishinaabe agreed to share some land but kept special areas called reserves to continue their traditions. https://www.thecanadianencyclopedia.ca/en/article/treaty-3

Why Is Treaty 3 Important?

Treaty 3 reminds us of the promise to share the land and respect each other. It’s important because it shows how people can work together and respect each other’s cultures.

Did you know? Treaty 3 covers a huge area in Canada near lakes and rivers where many Anishinaabe people still live today.
